1. Purpose of This Note
This note is meant to help the Google Partner / implementation partner align their technical scope with the intended HKHT AI for Seva architecture for WF-005 Online Communications / Digital Reception Desk.
This is not a rejection of current work. This is not adding random new scope. It is a clarification of the intended architecture so that the partner can classify, phase, strengthen, and deliver the implementation plan correctly.
The latest WF-005 Document B should be treated as the primary workflow-specific 30-Day Production-Ready Implementation Brief. This note explains how to read that brief alongside GE Technical Scope, GE Gap Analysis, , Important Upgrades, KCKE/Media AI boundaries, and workflow transcript evidence.
Evidence basis: Latest WF-005 Document B sections 1A-1F; GE Technical Scope; GE Gap Analysis; .
2. Core Understanding Expected from the Partner
WF-005 should become the Digital Reception Desk module of the Hyderabad AI Revenue & Seva Growth Platform.
It should not be reduced to a small isolated demo or disconnected reply automation.
It should be production-aligned from Day 1, even if early execution uses mock/sample data where dependencies are pending.
It must preserve common IDs, source tracking, approval gates, dashboard visibility, CRM/ERP readiness, and future multi-center scale.
It must capture online communication as relationship intelligence, not just message handling.
It must support staff-assist and human-governed sending for public, donor-sensitive, financial, spiritual, and high-risk communication.
Evidence basis: Latest WF-005 Document B sections 1, 1A, 1B, 6, 13; GE Technical Scope operating constraints.

The GE Gap Analysis clarifies the bigger intended architecture: WF-005 must remember important conversations across WhatsApp, email, website, social media, and calls; connect those communications to CRM; govern escalations; show pending/delayed/resolved status; and avoid scattered channel memory.

7. What the Partner Should Not Miss
Common IDs: Contact ID, Interaction/Ticket ID, Task ID, Owner ID, Approval ID, Source, Campaign/Event/Yatra/Donation IDs where relevant.
CRM is the relationship source of truth for contacts, donors, yatris, source tracking, follow-ups, and touchpoints.
ERP-ready fields are needed from Day 1 even if ERP integration comes later.
Approval gates are mandatory for donor-sensitive, financial, public, devotional/philosophical, deity/media, HR/legal/procurement, and exception decisions.
Audit logs must show AI draft, human edit, approval, sent/resolved status, and owner/action history.
Dashboard visibility must be operational and leadership-facing, not only chat output.
Role ownership must be validated; transcript-derived names are evidence, not final routing truth.
Fallback procedures are needed for missing data, failed APIs, and wrong AI output.
An API registry should classify every external connector and dependency.
Data quality checks should cover duplicates, missing source, missing owner, stale follow-up, and unresolved approvals.
Golden journey testing is required before production sign-off.
KCKE and Media AI boundaries must remain clear.
Future multi-center packaging should be protected without promising full rollout by Day 30.
Evidence basis: Latest Document B sections 1E, 11, 13, appendices; GE Gap Analysis; KCKE/Media AI files.
