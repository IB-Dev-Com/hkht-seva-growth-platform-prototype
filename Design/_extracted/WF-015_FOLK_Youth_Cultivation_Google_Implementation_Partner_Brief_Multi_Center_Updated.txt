1. Executive Partner Brief
WF-015 FOLK Youth Cultivation should be designed as a multi-center, AI-assisted, CRM-aligned, KCKE-supported, dashboard-visible, human-governed youth cultivation system. The workflow should track each youth from first contact through programs, reading groups, mentor touchpoints, sadhana progression, seva engagement, trips/Yatras, and long-term preacher/devotee/congregation development.
Source basis: Earlier WF-015 blueprint; revised 30-Day Production Brief; Mumbai FOLK transcript; Google Partner Scope Alignment Note; Gap Analysis and Important Upgrades documents.
Topic | Updated Brief
Strategic role | FOLK is a long-term youth-to-devotee/preacher pipeline, not only an event attendance workflow.
Multi-center requirement | The same platform must support centralized center models and distributed city-node models such as Mumbai’s five-center structure.
Production mindset | Day-7 and Day-14 work can use sample/mock data, but must preserve production-shaped IDs, data paths, approvals, dashboards, and integration contracts.
Primary design shift | Move from isolated program follow-up to relationship intelligence: full seeker timeline, mentor memory, sadhana signals, dormant contact reactivation, and center-wise dashboards.
Partner ask | Provide TRD, solution architecture, Google product mapping, integration architecture, costing, risks, deployment plan, and phased rollout plan.

2. Updated Current Workflow Understanding
The current FOLK workflow is distributed across human relationships, programs, apps, CRMs, WhatsApp/calls, attendance systems, sadhana tracking, online courses, and mentor memory. It is functional, but not yet a unified relationship intelligence system.
Current Element | Updated Understanding
Primary trigger | A youth enters through book/pamphlet distribution, online course/webinar, paid social ad, event registration, referral, reading group, college/hostel outreach, or festival.
Immediate action | Usually invite or call the youth to a weekend/daily program, online reading group, course, or local center session.
People involved | FOLK heads, local guides, volunteers, callers, presenters, sadhana/attendance coordinators, data/app coordinators, and leadership reviewers. Exact owner/approver/backup requires validation [Owner: NV].
Current tools | FOLK CRM, DMT CRM, Prabhupada World / e-learning platform, sadhana trackers, no-code web apps, event registration pages, Google tools, WhatsApp, calls, and reports. Exact APIs/export formats require validation [API: NV].
Current reports | Attendance, lead source, follow-up, sadhana, ashram level, potential preacher, residency/center strength, events and program performance. Final standard dashboard definition requires validation [Data: NV].
Current gap | The mentor may know the youth’s real interest, inclination, enthusiasm, family/career concerns and spiritual doubts, but this knowledge may not be captured in the system.

3. Multi-Center Evidence: What Changed After Mumbai Input
The Mumbai transcript confirms the earlier design but adds important multi-center requirements. These should be treated as platform design requirements, not late scope changes.
Mumbai Evidence | Required Platform Implication
Distributed center model | Mumbai does not operate only as one centralized weekend-stay model; it has separate programs in multiple city centers. The system must support Center_Node_ID and local program nodes.
Online + offline cultivation | Mumbai uses online courses, webinars, community e-learning, and daily online reading groups, but physical touch through trips, festivals, or center visits remains important.
Multiple CRMs/apps | DMT CRM, Prabhupada World, FOLK CRM, sadhana trackers and no-code apps already exist. The first architecture should support mapping/integration, not forced replacement.
Individual follow-up frequency | Different youth need different follow-up intensity. Some can attend daily; others feel pressured by weekly follow-up. AI must recommend frequency, not blindly automate reminders.
Drop-off psychology | Drop-off can happen due to fear of detachment, family/friend pressure, misunderstanding, guilt from not following, or avoidance of guide calls.
Mentor memory | Mentors know details that are not in the database. The system must capture structured mentor notes without weakening trust.
Long-term cultivation | A youth may return after years. The platform must support dormant contact reactivation and long-term relationship memory.

5. Platform Compatibility Requirements
Requirement | Partner Design Rule
Identity discipline | Use Common Contact ID as root identity; link FOLK Seeker ID, Donor ID, Yatri ID, Campaign ID, Event ID, Course ID, Guide ID, Task ID where relevant.
CRM/DBMS boundary | CRM/DBMS stores contacts, source attribution, follow-ups, touchpoints, relationship timeline, stages, mentor notes, and relationship status.
ERP boundary | ERP stores validated financial/operational records such as payments, receipts, inventory, vouchers, procurement, finance, HR and approvals. WF-015 should only link to ERP IDs when relevant.
KCKE boundary | KCKE is for source-grounded Krishna conscious content, Prabhupada references, presentation support, philosophical replies and devotional content. It must not store contact status, payment status, attendance or task status.
Media AI boundary | Media AI may support reels, presentations, storyboards and event/yatra/festival content, but must not become CRM, ERP or source of spiritual truth.
Gemini Enterprise boundary | Workspace productivity, drafting, summaries, Drive search, meeting support, and internal assistant capabilities.
Vertex AI / Agent Builder | Scalable agents, classification, recommendation, routing, prediction, graph/RAG integration and future production-grade orchestration.
BigQuery / Looker Studio | Leadership dashboards, center comparisons, data quality intelligence, progression trends, and future predictive analytics.
ClickUp / task layer | Task execution and accountability layer where selected by HKHT. Exact integration status [API: NV].
WF-013 readiness | FOLK configuration templates, center model types, standard fields, dashboard templates and training kit must be designed for future replication.

10. Human Approval and Governance Requirements
Governance Area | Requirement
AI can do | Draft, classify, summarize, score, recommend, route, alert, prepare dashboards and propose next action.
AI cannot finalize | Sensitive spiritual guidance, counseling, public devotional/philosophical content, donor-sensitive messages, financial/payment/receipt decisions, high-risk exceptions.
Guide Support Copilot boundary | Use mentor-approved notes and human review first. Automatic recording/real-time coaching requires separate governance and explicit scope approval [Scope: NV].
Access control | Sensitive mentor notes must have role-based access; not every coordinator should see all youth-sensitive context.
Routing validation | Accountable owner, performer, approver, backup, escalation person, dashboard reviewer and data steward must be validated before automation routes go live [Owner: NV].
Audit trail | All AI-generated suggestions, human approvals, follow-up tasks and stage changes should be logged.
