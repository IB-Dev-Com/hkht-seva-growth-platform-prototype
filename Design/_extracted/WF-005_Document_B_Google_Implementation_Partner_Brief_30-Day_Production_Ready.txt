1B. Production Readiness Definition
For WF-005, production readiness does not mean full enterprise completion or full ERP integration. It means a live, controlled, human-governed Digital Reception Desk module that selected users can operate with real or validated data paths, visible dashboards, accountable ownership, and fallback procedures.
Source basis: Generated WF-005 Blueprint sections 7-18; Project_Glossary.txt; KCKE_Media_CRM_Dependency_Map.docx; Workflow_to_Data_Dependency_Map_Updated.xlsx; HKHT_AI_Transformation_Analysis (1).html recommendations on production readiness, unified master data, role validation, command-center visibility, and failure/fallback protocol.
Readiness criterion | WF-005 production-ready meaning
Live operational usage | Selected users process real or approved sample enquiries through one controlled queue; no external auto-send unless governance approves category-level automation.
Validated data flow | Each interaction produces Interaction ID, Contact ID/provisional contact, source, category, owner, status, next action, approval status, and audit trail.
Dashboard visibility | Leadership, operational, revenue/conversion, data quality, and AI performance dashboards show backlog, delays, lead creation, exceptions, and Mukund-level decisions.
Approval routing | Donor-sensitive, financial, public review, complaint, booking, devotional/philosophical, and high-risk replies require human approval.
Exception handling | Unclassified, high-risk, overdue, API-failed, or unclear-owner cases route to named escalation paths with backup persons.
Role ownership | Owner, performer, approver, backup, escalation person, dashboard reviewer, and data custodian are validated before production routing.
Data quality checks | Duplicate contact check, missing source, missing owner, missing next action, outdated FAQ/template, and unresolved approval status are tracked.
AI-assisted execution | AI classifies, drafts, summarizes, scores urgency/revenue intent, and recommends routing; humans control final high-risk communication.
Cross-workflow compatibility | Donation/Yatra/membership/booking/follow-up enquiries hand off to WF-001/WF-002/WF-003/WF-006/WF-007/WF-009/WF-012/WF-014 with common IDs and source tracking.
Fallback process | Manual intake and manual sending remain available when API, AI, or channel integration fails; outages are logged and shown on the dashboard.
Golden journey testing | Test at least five end-to-end journeys, including Google review complaint, WhatsApp donation enquiry, Yatra enquiry, spiritual question, and booking/seva request.
Measurable improvement | Track response time reduction, backlog reduction, lead capture increase, follow-up completion, AI draft acceptance, and Mukund Prabhu dependency reduction.
