Production Readiness Definition
For WF-001, production readiness does not mean every future ERP, voice, WhatsApp, payment, or media integration is fully live by Day 30. It means the workflow has a stable production base, real operational users, validated data flow, human approval gates, dashboard visibility, documented fallback paths, and a clear integration contract for the larger platform.
Readiness criterion | WF-001 production-ready definition
Live operational usage | At least one Yatra or pilot batch is operated through the command center with selected live users and controlled real data where approved.
Validated data flow | Lead, yatri, registration, payment status, document readiness, task, feedback, and donor handoff records flow through defined tables/fields with stable IDs.
Dashboard visibility | Leadership and operations dashboards show registrations, seats, payments, missing documents, tasks, escalations, campaign source, and P&L indicators where available.

AI-assisted execution | AI drafts content, follow-up, summaries, checklists, scripts, and exception notes, but final decisions remain human-governed.
Cross-workflow compatibility | WF-001 records can connect to WF-003 leads, WF-002 call status, WF-007 donor cultivation, WF-009 finance, and WF-006 CRM master data.
Fallback process | Manual fallback exists for Sheets/AppSheet, CRM/API, WhatsApp, voice, payment, dashboard, and on-ground execution if automation or integration fails.
