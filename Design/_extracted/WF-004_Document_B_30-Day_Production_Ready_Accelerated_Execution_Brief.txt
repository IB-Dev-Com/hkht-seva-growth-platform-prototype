1B. Production Readiness Definition
For WF-004, production readiness means that the content request, creation, review, approval, publication-readiness, archive, and performance-feedback path can be used by selected HKHT users in live operational conditions, even if some integrations remain staged, semi-automated, or API-gated.
Minimum production-readiness criteria for Day 30:
Live operational usage: selected departments can submit real content requests through the approved intake path, not only sample requests.
Validated data flow: each request receives Request ID, Campaign/Event/Yatra/Seva link where applicable, owner, performer, reviewer, final approver, backup, SLA, approval status, source, created date, last action, and next action.
Dashboard visibility: leadership and workflow owners can see request volume, bottlenecks, pending approvals, delayed campaign assets, asset readiness, and content risk flags.
Approval routing: devotional/philosophical, donor-sensitive, public communication, deity/media, financial-detail, CSR/HNI, and exception content cannot bypass human approval.
Exception handling: incomplete briefs, missing source references, urgent campaign dates, low-confidence asset retrieval, copyright/rights issues, and overdue approvals route to the right owner and escalation person.
Role ownership: current owner, performer, reviewer, final approver, backup, dashboard reviewer, data custodian, and Mukund Prabhu dependency must be validated before automated routing is treated as live.
AI-assisted execution: AI may generate drafts, summaries, variants, briefs, checklists, and recommendations; it must not directly publish or send sensitive content without human approval.
Cross-workflow compatibility: WF-004 output must be usable by Digital Marketing, Yatra Lifecycle, Donor/LLP/CSR, Website/Landing Page, Community Outreach, Digital Reception Desk, KCKE, Media AI, and future ERP/CRM systems.
Fallback process: if an agent, integration, dashboard, or approval automation fails, the workflow must continue through a defined manual path using the same IDs and approval records.
Golden journey testing: at least one campaign-content journey should be tested end-to-end from request to approved content bundle to publishing-ready status to dashboard visibility and CRM/campaign linkage.

1E. Cross-Workflow Integration and Platform Safeguards
WF-004 must not become a disconnected content tool. It must share identity, campaign, task, approval, and analytics structures with the wider Hyderabad AI Revenue & Seva Growth Platform.
Shared IDs: Contact ID, Donor ID, Yatri ID, Community ID, Family ID, Campaign ID, Event ID, Yatra ID, Seva ID, Asset ID, Request ID, Task ID, Approval ID, and Source ID must be used where relevant.
CRM boundary: CRM/DBMS is the relationship source of truth for contacts, donors, yatris, families, communities, segments, campaign source, and follow-up status.
ERP boundary: ERP is the future structured operational and financial system of record. WF-004 must store ERP-ready fields but must not create unstable financial handoffs or invent payment/receipt truth.
KCKE boundary: KCKE is used only for source-grounded Krishna conscious content, donor narratives, philosophical replies, preaching, education, and devotional content. It is not used for contact status, payment status, task status, inventory, approvals, or finance records.
Media AI boundary: Media AI handles reels, videos, scene-level storyboards, approved visual storytelling, raw footage retrieval, deity/character repositories, audio layers, and final media governance. It may consume KCKE output but remains separate from KCKE and Gemini Enterprise productivity agents.
ClickUp/task layer: where execution governance is already routed through ClickUp or another task layer, WF-004 must create task IDs, owners, due dates, approval tasks, and escalation records without replacing the task system unnecessarily.
Leadership visibility: WF-004 must contribute to the leadership visibility layer by showing campaign content readiness, delayed approvals, risk flags, production throughput, and decisions requiring senior review.
API dependency tracking: confirmed APIs, unconfirmed APIs, file/Drive access, CRM paths, WhatsApp/template paths, Media AI paths, KCKE paths, and future ERP paths must be tracked in a consolidated API registry.
Fallback workflow: if integration fails, the content team must still execute through the approved manual path while preserving Request ID, owner, approval status, final asset link, and source/campaign metadata.

5. Current Bottlenecks and Productivity Loss
Manual work: Requirement clarification, brief rewriting, channel variant creation, Drive searching, status tracking, and approval chasing are manual-heavy.
Follow-up leakage: Requesters and approvers can lose track of iterations, comments, and deadlines when approvals happen outside a structured workflow.
Data fragmentation: Content, approvals, assets, source references, campaign links, and performance data are not yet one structured data model.
WhatsApp/spreadsheet dependency: Useful for communication, but weak for audit trail, version control, SLA, and enterprise memory.
Mukund Prabhu dependency: Senior involvement is needed because content is public-facing, donor-sensitive, devotional, and cross-departmental; this creates managerial load if not dashboard-governed.
Revenue leakage: Late campaign assets can delay donation/Yatra/community campaign launches and reduce available conversion window.
Missed preaching/donor/Yatra opportunity: Past approved content, festival significance, donor stories, and Yatra media are not easily reused at scale.

8.1. Content Intake & Brief Agent
Purpose: Converts requests into structured, complete, platform-compatible content briefs.
Users: Requesters, Satish/content manager, department coordinators.
Inputs: Form/AppSheet entry, campaign objective, audience, language, channel, deadline, source material.
Outputs: Brief ID, missing-field list, priority, routed task, campaign/source tags.
Data needed: Department, requester, content type, campaign/event/Yatra/seva, deadline, owner, approval route.
Tools/connectors: Google Forms/AppSheet/Sheets, Drive, Gemini Workspace; later workflow DB/API.
Human approval points: Human content manager validates high-priority or incomplete briefs.
Escalation logic: Incomplete/urgent briefs route to content manager; campaign/revenue content routes to Mukund Prabhu/dashboard reviewer if SLA risk.
Success metrics: Brief completeness, intake-to-assignment time, rework caused by unclear brief.
8.2. Campaign Bundle Planner Agent
Purpose: Turns one campaign or Yatra/festival brief into a full content bundle.
Users: Content manager, digital marketing, fundraising, Yatra team.
Inputs: Approved campaign brief, timeline, CTA, donor/yatri segment, channels.
Outputs: Bundle checklist: posters, reels, WhatsApp, SMS, email, captions, ad copy, landing-page copy, donor collateral.
Data needed: Campaign ID, Yatra ID/Event ID/Seva ID, audience, languages, channel specs.
Tools/connectors: Sheets/AppSheet, Calendar, Drive, Gemini; later CRM/campaign API.
Human approval points: Human approves bundle scope before production.
Escalation logic: If campaign launch date is at risk, escalate to owner and dashboard reviewer.
Success metrics: Bundle completeness, on-time campaign launch, assets per campaign.
8.3. KCKE Grounding Agent
Purpose: Retrieves source-backed KC references and flags unsupported spiritual claims.
Users: Writers, philosophical reviewers, education/preaching content team.
Inputs: Topic, festival, yatra place, seva, audience level, language.
Outputs: Source-backed points, exact references, confidence flags, review warnings.
Data needed: KCKE source nodes, authority tier, verse/chapter/timestamp, audience tags.
Tools/connectors: KCKE RAG/vector/graph, Gemini/Vertex; not operational CRM/ERP data.
Human approval points: Philosophical reviewer approves before public use.
Escalation logic: Unsupported/contradictory/high-risk claims route to senior reviewer.
Success metrics: Percent outputs with exact sources, reviewer rejection rate, unsupported claim count.
8.4. Copy & Variant Agent
Purpose: Drafts channel-specific and multilingual content variants.
Users: Content writers, digital marketing team, donor/yatri communication owners.
Inputs: Brief, KCKE references, approved CTA, channel specs, tone, language.
Outputs: WhatsApp, SMS, email, Instagram caption, ad copy, YouTube description, script variants.
Data needed: Campaign/source IDs, audience, language, CTA, approved donation/Yatra links.
Tools/connectors: Gemini Enterprise/Workspace, Docs/Sheets; later Vertex AI and CRM segmentation.
Human approval points: Human review before publishing or sending.
Escalation logic: Donor-sensitive/financial/philosophical variants route to dedicated approver.
Success metrics: Draft acceptance rate, variants/hour, iteration count, conversion lift.
8.5. Media Asset Retrieval Agent

Users: Designers, video editors, content manager.

Outputs: Candidate assets with thumbnails, metadata, rights/approval status, source links.
Data needed: Asset ID, approval status, rights, location, festival, deity/person, aspect ratio, tags.
Tools/connectors: Drive/GCS, metadata DB, vector search; later Media AI repository.
